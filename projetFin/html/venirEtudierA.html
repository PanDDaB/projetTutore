STUDY AT IUT

Options for studying in France

All courses at the IUT in Lens are taught in French. A good level of understanding of the French language is therefore essential. For DUT and bachelor courses, level B2 is required. This corresponds to the target level for the baccalaureate test. The Ministry of National Education and Higher Education defines this level of competence as follows: "Can understand the essentials of a concrete or abstract subject in a complex text, including a technical discussion in his/her specialty. He/she can communicate with a degree of spontaneity and fluency, such as a conversation with a native speaker. The student can give an opinion on a current subject and is able to discuss.

The IUT offers diplomas in 4 streams accessible to foreign students either to come and study in training on an individual basis, or as part of an agreement signed with a partner establishment of the IUT for targeted training.

Validate your level of competence in French

Those not of French nationality wishing to have their French language skills certified for personal or professional purposes have the possibility of taking diplomas or tests which assess their level. The Lille Nord de France University organized DELF-DALF exams created by the Ministry of National Education in Lille for the 2009-2010 academic year.

The six diplomas which constitute DELF and DALF are completely independent. Thus, the candidate, depending on his/her level, can register directly for the exam of his/her choice. At each level, four skills are assessed: oral comprehension and oral production, written comprehension and written production.

DELF : Diplôme d’Etudes en Langue Française (Diploma of French Language Studies)

  • DELF A1: completion of initial acquisition
  • DELF A2: «get by» elementary level
  • DELF B1: threshold level
  • DELF B2: linguistic autonomy

Le DALF : Diplôme Approfondi de Langue Française (Diploma of Advanced French)

  • DALF C2 : master
