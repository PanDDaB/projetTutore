STUDYING ABROAD

Studying abroad

Students have the option of studying abroad for one semester during their second DUT year or during the final year of their degree course.

Applications must be made during the year before the study period abroad.

More info about how to apply

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

IUT’s international academic partners

UQAC = University of Québec at Chicoutimi – bilateral arrangement

UQAC welcomes students for one semester, which is counted as one semester at IUT. Every student candidate must select a set of 5 courses to be completed abroad. The validation of the semester at Chicoutimi validates the equivalent semester at IUT.

  • Eligibility: DUT2 semester 4 students from all IUT departments
  • Number of students: three places, with additional places available in some years
  • Teaching language: French
  • Duration of stay: beginning of January to end of April
Site web de l’Université du Québec à Chicoutimi

CREPUQ-BCI = Association of Recteurs Et Principaux of Universities in Québec

CREPUQ brings together the universities of Quebec, mainly French-speaking, but also one English-speaking university. A student wishing to complete an academic semester at one of the universities of the CREPUQ-BCI program must submit an application file according to a specific procedure with the International Relations Service at the University of Artois during the year preceding his/her departure. After IUT approval, the file is forwarded to the recipient university, which may accept or refuse the candidate.

  • Eligibility: DUT2 semester 4 students from all IUT departments
  • Number of students: unlimited
  • Teaching language: French or English
  • Duration of stay: beginning of January to end of April
CREPUQ- BCI website

The Institute of Technology Sligo is located in the north west of Ireland

L’institut technologique de Sligo se situe au nord-ouest de l’Irlande.

  • Eligibity: students on bachelor courses in the fields of tourism: marketing of products connected with tourism, marketing of tourist, accommodation, cultural and sport infrastructures.
  • Number of students: three
  • Teaching language: English level B2
  • Duration of stay: beginning of January to end of April
Site web Institute of Technology Sligo

Institute of Technology Dundalk – Erasmus+ Programme

The Institute of Technology Dundalk is located in the north east of Ireland.

  • Eligibility: DUT INFO or MMI students and those on bachelor courses in the fields of tourism: marketing of products connected with tourism, marketing of tourist, accommodation, cultural and sport infrastructures.
  • Number of students: one INFO & MMI student and one LP CITHECS student
  • Teaching language: English level B2
  • Duration of stay: beginning of January to end of April
Site web Dundalk Institute of Technology

University Pablo Olavide – Séville (Spain) - Programme Erasmus+

  • Eligibility: DUT TC and GEA
  • Number of students: two
  • Teaching language: Spanish level B1
  • Duration of stay: beginning of January to end of April
Pablo Olavide University, Seville website

Internships abroad

Students can do their 2nd year internship or bachelor degree in the form of a graduation project at a university laboratory of a partner institution. In this case:

  • DUT 1 students apply in June for an internship to be completed in the 2nd year.
  • Bachelor degree students apply in September for their end-of-year internship

Students also have the opportunity of finding an internship abroad themselves.

Advices for a future departure abroad

Which students are eligible for a placement abroad?

Eligibility: first-year students as part of a short-term internship. Students need to find their internship independently.

Students in their second year of DUT or bachelor degree not on a work-study programme can take advantage of IUT’s contacts.

These students can also find an internship abroad independently.

How to apply

Regular information meetings enable students to keep informed of the steps required, which may vary from country to country.

The IUT of Lens helps and supports students in completing applications and in preparing their stay.

Registering an application file ►

Language requirements

Language levels required may vary according to location. The references indicated for each location correspond to the criteria of the European Framework of Reference for languages ► Cadre Européen de Référence pour les langues ►

Language teachers from different departments help students assess their level.

La Maison des Langues de l’Université d’Artois ► offers free improvement workshops and level tests to all students of the University.

Financial assistance

There is a range of different financial aids to help students achieve their mobility for studies or internships abroad. The eligibility conditions and the amounts vary according to the funding.

More information regarding financial help and the conditions of application are available from the Service des Relations Internationales de l’Université d’Artois ►
